# Slide 2.4 fact-check
Date: 17 May 2026

## Summary

Four of five claims hold. One was materially wrong and has been corrected
in `content.js`. One is soft and has been softened.

## Claim-by-claim

### 1. "543 GW added by China in 2024 alone."  CONFIRMED
- National Energy Administration (NEA) figure, cited by multiple outlets.
- Breakdown: ~277 GW solar (+45%), ~80 GW wind (+18%), balance from
  thermal, hydro, nuclear.
- Total Chinese installed capacity reached ~3,349 GW end of 2024
  (+14.6% YoY).
- Sources: Enerdata, China Energy and Transport (CET), Statista, EIA.

### 2. "More than the US has added in its history."  FALSE - CORRECTED
- US total installed electric generating capacity: ~1,250 GW (2024).
  China added 543 GW in a single year, which is less than half the US
  lifetime build-out, not more.
- The defensible comparisons:
  - China added ~10x what the US added the same year (~56 GW in 2024).
  - China's 2024 build-out was ~12% larger than India's entire
    installed capacity.
- **Action taken:** changed slide text to
  "roughly ten times what the US added the same year."
  The 10x comparison is more rhetorically punchy than the (incorrect)
  lifetime comparison anyway.

### 3. "~400 GW projected spare Chinese capacity by 2030."  CONFIRMED
- Goldman Sachs forecast, "about three times the world's expected
  data-center power demand at that time."
- **Action taken:** added the "~3x global data-center demand
  (Goldman Sachs)" attribution so the number lands with a source
  visible on slide.

### 4. "Feb 2026, Chinese models passed US models in weekly token
       usage on OpenRouter for the first time."  CONFIRMED
- Week of 9-15 Feb 2026: Chinese models 4.12T tokens vs US 2.94T tokens.
- Week of 16-22 Feb 2026: Chinese 5.16T (+127% in three weeks),
  US dropped to 2.7T.
- Top 5 models by token volume: 4 of 5 Chinese (MiniMax M2.5,
  Kimi K2.5, GLM-5, DeepSeek V3.2).
- Sources: CGTN, TrendingTopics, Dataconomy, AICost.

### 5. "Power is 60 to 70 percent of model operating costs."  WEAK
- Direct support for the specific 60-70% figure is thin.
- What is solidly supported:
  - "Power is the single largest component of ongoing operating cost
    for an LLM service."
  - GPUs account for >60% of data-center energy use (a related but
    different metric).
- **Action taken:** softened to
  "Power is the single largest line in inference operating cost,"
  which is well-supported and survives Q and A.

## Net change to the slide

- Hero value 1 (543 GW) label updated.
- Hero value 2 (Feb 2026 OpenRouter) unchanged, was already correct.
- Secondary value (400 GW) gained a Goldman Sachs attribution.
- Column B support line softened from "60 to 70 percent" to
  "the single largest line."
- Speaker notes updated to mark fact-check complete and dated.
